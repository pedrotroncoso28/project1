The Big Bang

In the beginning, there was nothing. There was no space or time. Everything we that exits today was concentrated in an infinitely small and dense point known as a singularity...
About 13.8 billion years ago, that singularity exploded. A violent and extremely rapid expansion began. This is what we call the Big Bang. In fractions of a second, the universe inflated exponentially in a period called inflation. Gradually, it began to cool enough for energy to be transformed into particles like protons, neutrons, and electrons.
